Uniform Resource Locator

What is a URL?

A URL (Uniform Resource Locator) is the address used to locate resources on the internet such as web pages, images, and files.

Example: https://www.google.com

Structure of a URL

  • Protocol: https://
  • Domain Name: example.com
  • Subdomain: www.
  • Path: /images/photo.jpg
  • Parameters: ?id=10

Importance of URLs

  • Help locate websites easily
  • Allow access to specific pages
  • Enable sharing of online resources

Rules of URLs

  • No spaces allowed
  • Must be typed correctly
  • Case sensitivity may apply
